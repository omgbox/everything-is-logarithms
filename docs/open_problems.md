# Open Problems

## The Log Riemann Hypothesis

**Problem**: Is there a natural operator whose eigenvalues are the non-trivial zeros of zeta(s)?

The logarithmic framework suggests that zeta(s) should be viewed as a trace or determinant in some appropriate space.

## The Logarithmic Cohomology Conjecture

**Problem**: Does log-cohomology detect the multiplicative structure of varieties?

For a smooth projective variety X, the log-cohomology groups H^n_log(X) should capture the multiplicative invariants of X.

## The Log Fixed Point Theorem

**Problem**: Under what conditions does every contraction of log-space have a unique fixed point?

The Banach fixed point theorem holds for contractions in standard metric spaces. The logarithmic analog should hold for "multiplicative contractions."

## The Logarithmic Dimension Conjecture

**Problem**: Can we define dimension for infinite-dimensional logarithmic objects?

The framework currently works only for finite-dimensional objects. Extending to infinite dimensions (e.g., p-adic integers Z_p) is an open challenge.

## The Tropical Connection Conjecture

**Problem**: Is tropical geometry a shadow of the logarithmic category?

The semiring (R, max, +) is isomorphic to (R+, *, +) via the logarithm. This suggests tropical geometry is the "real" structure being captured.
